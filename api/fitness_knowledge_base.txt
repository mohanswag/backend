### FLEXAI FITNESS KNOWLEDGE BASE ###

[EXERCISES - UPPER BODY]
1. Bench Press: Targets Pectoralis Major, Triceps, Anterior Deltoids. 
   - Execution: Lie on bench, grip bar slightly wider than shoulders, lower to mid-chest, press up.
   - Tip: Keep feet flat on floor for stability.
2. Pull-Ups: Targets Latissimus Dorsi, Biceps, Trapezius.
   - Execution: Grip bar overhand, pull body up until chin clears bar, lower slowly.
   - Tip: Engage core to prevent swinging.
3. Overhead Press: Targets Deltoids, Triceps, Upper Chest.
   - Execution: Standing or seated, press barbell/dumbbells from shoulders to full lockout above head.
4. Bicep Curls: Targets Biceps Brachii.
   - Execution: Hold weights at sides, curl towards shoulders while keeping elbows pinned to ribs.

[EXERCISES - LOWER BODY]
1. Squats: Targets Quadriceps, Glutes, Hamstrings, Core.
   - Execution: Feet shoulder-width apart, lower hips as if sitting in a chair, keep chest up, return to standing.
2. Deadlifts: Targets Posterior Chain (Hamstrings, Glutes, Lower Back).
   - Execution: Grip bar on floor, lift by extending hips and knees, keep bar close to shins.
3. Lunges: Targets Quads and Glutes.
   - Execution: Step forward and lower back knee towards floor, alternating legs.

[DIET & NUTRITION]
1. Muscle Gain (Bulking): Requires a calorie surplus (300-500 kcal above maintenance).
   - Macros: High protein (1.6g-2.2g/kg), moderate fats, high complex carbs.
   - Foods: Chicken breast, Paneer, Eggs, Quinoa, Oats, Peanut Butter.
2. Fat Loss (Cutting): Requires a calorie deficit (300-500 kcal below maintenance).
   - Macros: High protein (to preserve muscle), lower carbs, healthy fats.
   - Foods: Leafy greens, Fish, Berries, Greek Yogurt, Broccoli.
3. Hydration: Drink 3-4 liters of water daily for optimal muscle recovery.

[SUPPLEMENTS]
1. Whey Protein: Fast-digesting protein for post-workout recovery.
2. Creatine Monohydrate: Increases ATP production for strength and power.
3. Caffeine: Pre-workout stimulant for focus and energy.

[RECOVERY]
- Sleep: 7-9 hours of quality sleep is mandatory for muscle growth.
- Active Recovery: Light walking or stretching on rest days helps blood flow and reduces soreness.
